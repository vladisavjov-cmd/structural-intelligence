# What Structural Intelligence Really Is: Logic, Answerability, and the Internal Operation of Judgment

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
This paper argues that Structural Intelligence is best understood not only as a philosophical framework or style of analysis, but as a logic of judgment. The practical problem is that humans, institutions, and AI systems can all produce polished coherence faster than they produce grounded judgment, so what sounds complete is easily mistaken for what is answerable to reality. The paper gathers the central SI distinctions into a single spine—coherence and contact, answerability and defense, revision and performance, burden and appearance—and defines SI as the capacity to perceive, test, and navigate whether a form truly holds under pressure or only appears to hold. It then clarifies the philosophical floor beneath that definition by treating structure as patterned stabilization of relation within an already differentiated field, and local structurations as the formations that actually carry burden, consequence, meaning, or correction over time.
