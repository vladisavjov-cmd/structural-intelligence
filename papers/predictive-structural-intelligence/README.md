# Predictive Structural Intelligence: Breach Hazard, Buffer Exhaustion, and the Time-Structure of Answerability

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
This paper develops a predictive architecture for Structural Intelligence by extending the framework from diagnosis toward time-bound breach-hazard estimation. It asks when a structure that appears to hold may cross from managed strain into forced contact, and translates SI concepts into rate-sensitive variables such as drift velocity, contact half-life, contradiction recovery lag, repair velocity, maintenance inflation, burden export velocity, hidden-holder depletion, hysteresis penalty, synthetic trace discount, and binding strength. The paper does not claim deterministic collapse prediction, but a disciplined predictive layer answerable to evidence, downgrade rules, falsifiers, and time.

### Keywords
Structural Intelligence; predictive SI; breach hazard; buffer exhaustion; answerability; hidden holders; burden export; hysteresis; synthetic trace; resilience engineering; early-warning signals; system safety
