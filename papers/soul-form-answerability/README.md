# Soul, Form, and Answerability: A Structural Reading Framework

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
This paper argues that soul does not need to be treated as mystical vagueness or discarded language. Read structurally, soul names the depth-dimension of a life that remains answerable beyond its current roles, self-image, and outward coherence. The paper distinguishes soul from persona, psyche, and self-concept, and introduces the invisible trace, the thinness threshold, and vertical answerability to explain why deeper truth often becomes visible when ordinary forms can no longer carry grief, vocation, conscience, creativity, repair, or moral injury. It also argues that a person can remain socially functional while becoming inwardly false, and closes by asking what happens when AI imitates the language of depth without bearing embodiment, continuity, grief, or answerable life-history.

### Keywords
soul; selfhood; philosophy of mind; personal identity; authenticity; grief; vocation; creativity; answerability; artificial intelligence; technology and society
