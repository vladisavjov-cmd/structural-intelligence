# A Mathematical Grammar of Structure: Adequacy, Pilot Severance, and Collapse Survivability

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Journal article  
**Version:** Latest archived (Apr 2026)

### Abstract
This paper develops a mathematical grammar for describing how structure holds, fails, and survives under pressure. It focuses on adequacy, pilot severance, and collapse survivability as core variables for understanding whether a formation can continue to organize relation when burden rises, steering weakens, or contact exceeds its available capacity. The aim is not formalism for its own sake, but a more exact account of when a structure remains viable, when it becomes severed from answerable steering, and what conditions allow survival or reorganization after collapse pressure arrives.

### Keywords
structure; adequacy; pilot severance; collapse survivability; viability; reorganization; answerability; contact; collapse; structural intelligence
