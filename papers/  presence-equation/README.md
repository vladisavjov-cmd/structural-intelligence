# The Presence Equation: A Formal Phenomenology of Reality-Contact in the AI Era

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (Feb 2026)

### Abstract
In an era where coherence can be generated cheaply—by institutions, media systems, and AI—“sounding true” has separated from being in contact with reality. This paper offers a formal phenomenology of presence: a structural model that describes when a human system is actually answerable to constraint, capable of revision, and resistant to programmability, versus when it is producing coherent outputs untethered from contact. The model is presented as a presence equation—not as a scientific law, but as a grammar of intelligibility: a way of naming the dependencies that make a life real, corrigible, and self-owning over time. The point is not prediction. The point is diagnosis: to clarify what presence is, how it is lost, how it is simulated, and what failure looks like when coherence replaces reality.
