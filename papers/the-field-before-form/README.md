# The Field Before Form: Difference, Gradient, and the Pre-Local Conditions of Structuration

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (Apr 2026)

### Abstract
This paper develops the field layer of Structural Intelligence by asking what kind of condition makes local structuration possible before any bounded form has stabilized. It argues that local forms do not arise from blankness, but precipitate from fields. In this framework, the field is not reality itself, not Being, and not a passive container. It is the differentiated condition within reality from which local forms can emerge: reality already charged by difference, asymmetry, limit, potential, pressure, and relation. The paper develops the role of difference, gradient, potential, metabolic cost, field capture, scale adequacy, and collapse in the formation and failure of structures. Its central claim is that field precedes form not necessarily in time, but in scope: every local holder emerges from a wider differentiated condition it never fully exhausts.
