# Persona, Shadow, and Cheap Coherence: A Jungian Map of the Psyche in the Digital Age (Read Through Structural Intelligence)

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** v1.0 (Feb 2026)

### Abstract
Carl Jung offered not a science of the psyche but a topology: a map of how inner life organizes itself into persona, shadow, projection, and individuation. This paper rereads that topology through Structural Intelligence in the AI era, where coherence is cheap and identity can be stabilized through performative legibility. Persona becomes not merely a social mask but an interface optimized for coherence-signals. Shadow becomes not merely disowned content but structural debt: what is excluded to keep the interface stable and acceptable. The paper argues that modern confusion is often not lack of information but surplus coherence without contact: fluent narratives that reduce anxiety while increasing insulation. It distinguishes integration from aesthetic “authenticity,” and treats individuation as a repair process: restoring answerability under constraint rather than polishing self-description. The aim is a Jungian map without mysticism—persona/shadow as structural functions under reward-pressure and cheap coherence.
