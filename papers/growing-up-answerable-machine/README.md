# Growing Up Answerable to a Machine: AI, Development, and the Formation of the Self

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
This paper argues that the central developmental risk of youth AI use is not only misinformation, addictive design, or unsafe advice. It is the gradual rerouting of answerability itself. In this view, development is not just skill acquisition or identity experimentation, but formation under friction, asymmetry, co-regulation, contradiction, repair, and lived consequence. When children and adolescents increasingly turn to AI for comfort, advice, reflection, companionship, and private self-interpretation, they may be forming a self in relation to a machine. The paper develops three diagnostic concepts—relational overfitting, semantic soothing without somatic maturation, and identity as prompt rather than deposited trace—and argues that repeated machine answerability can lower tolerance for contradiction, thin the capacity for repair, and reshape the kind of person being formed.

### Keywords
Structural Intelligence; youth AI; adolescence; answerability; relational overfitting; semantic soothing; somatic maturation; co-regulation; identity formation; deposited trace; machine companionship
