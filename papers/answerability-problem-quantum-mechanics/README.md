# The Answerability Problem in Quantum Mechanics: Observation, Interpretation, and Structural Closure Under Constraint

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Journal article  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
Quantum mechanics is extraordinarily successful in prediction, yet still unsettled at the level of interpretation. This paper argues that the core issue is not only technical or metaphysical, but answerability: predictive success has outrun ontological closure, and that gap invites explanations that promise more settlement than the formalism and evidence can really support. It separates several layers often collapsed under the term *observer*—physical interaction, measurement coupling, record formation, information update, and conscious witness—and uses that distinction to clarify disputes across Copenhagen, Many-Worlds, Bohmian mechanics, objective collapse, QBism, relational quantum mechanics, and consistent histories. The paper also argues that decoherence helps explain record stability and effective classicality without fully resolving the single-outcome problem. Its broader aim is to compare interpretations more honestly by asking what they explain, what they leave unresolved, and where they relocate their explanatory burden.
