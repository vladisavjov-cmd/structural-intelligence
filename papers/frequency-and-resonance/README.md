# Frequency and Resonance: Recurrence, Amplification, and the Dynamics of Field Formation

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (Apr 2026)

### Abstract
This paper develops the dynamic layer of the field within Structural Intelligence. It argues that fields do not only contain gradients and potentials; they also repeat, pulse, synchronize, amplify, and entrain. A field condition becomes structurally consequential when it returns strongly enough to shape local possibility. Repetition alone is not yet form. Repetition becomes formative when it resonates with a receptive structure, amplifies through fit, entrains nearby pathways, and crosses a threshold of stabilization. The paper develops frequency, resonance, entrainment, carrier frequency, dissonance, counter-frequency, threshold, hysteresis, and precipitation as concepts for understanding how fields become capable of producing local form. Its central claim is that local form emerges when recurring field-pressure resonates with available potential strongly enough to cross a threshold of holding.
