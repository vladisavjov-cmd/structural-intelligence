# The Field Before Form: Difference, Gradient, and the Pre-Local Conditions of Structuration

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (Apr 2026)

### Abstract
This paper develops the field layer of Structural Intelligence by asking what kind of condition makes local structuration possible before any particular structure can hold, fail, collapse, or revise. It argues that local forms do not arise from blankness, but precipitate from fields. In this framework, the field is not reality as such, not Being, not empty space, and not a passive container. The field is the differentiated condition within reality from which local forms can emerge: reality already charged by difference, asymmetry, limit, potential, pressure, and relation before those conditions stabilize into a bounded local holder. The paper develops a sequence from difference to field, gradient, potential, and local structuration. It also introduces field capture, scale adequacy, and the metabolic cost of gradients in order to explain why certain forms become dominant, why false structures can remain viable, and why collapse often follows the loss, reversal, or equalization of the gradient that sustained a form.
