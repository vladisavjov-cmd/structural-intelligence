# The Intelligence of Answerability: Why IQ, EQ, and SQ Are Not Enough

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Journal article  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
This paper proposes answerable intelligence as a distinct human capacity missing from ordinary intelligence discourse. A person may be cognitively bright, emotionally skillful, and spiritually articulate while still remain weakly revisable under contradiction, consequence, and self-implication. The paper argues that IQ, EQ, and SQ are real but insufficient because they do not isolate whether coherence remains answerable when truth becomes costly. Building on the Structural Intelligence framework, it defines answerable intelligence as the capacity to remain revisable under contact, develops binding revision as its decisive criterion, and explains why self-description, remorse, and insight often fail to produce actual correction. It also identifies major blockers of answerable intelligence, including persona protection, shame collapse, resonance treated as evidence, and the inability to survive self-implication without annihilation anxiety.
