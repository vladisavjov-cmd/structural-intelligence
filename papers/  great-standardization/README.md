# The Great Standardization: Genealogy of the Grid: Metrics, Platforms, and the Thinning of Answerability

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (Feb 2026)

### Abstract
Modern life did not merely become more rational. It became more standardized. This paper offers a philosophical genealogy of the grid: the drift by which human reality is increasingly translated into comparable units—scores, credentials, KPIs, dashboards, rankings, protocols, and platform signals. Standardization is not neutral description; it is an architectural act that reorganizes what counts as real, legible, and valuable. The gain is coordination power. The cost is structural: reality becomes what fits the grid, and what does not fit becomes invisible, punished, or forced into performance. The result is a civilization with high coherence and thinning contact, capable of optimizing itself while losing answerability. This paper names the mechanism: standardization converts contact into representation, judgment into compliance, and truth into audit. It does not condemn standardization; it clarifies its structure, its predictable corruptions, and its acceleration in the AI era.
