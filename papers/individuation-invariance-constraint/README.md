# Individuation as an Invariance Constraint: Building a Non-Programmable Self in the Age of Cheap Coherence

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** v1.0 (2026-03-11)

### Abstract
Individuation is reframed as the installation of invariances inside the self: non-tradable constraints that prevent identity from being rewritten by reward-pressure, belonging pressure, or narrative influence. Where coherence is cheap and contact is costly, the self becomes increasingly programmable by signals (approval, metrics, status), producing a stable surface and a drifting core. The paper proposes fixed worth as a structural floor (worth not priced by the room), and treats non-programmability as an operational outcome of binding commitments, irreversibility, and correction under contact.
