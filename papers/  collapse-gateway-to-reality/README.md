# Collapse as the Gateway to Reality: Why Presence Requires Containment

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** v1.0 (Feb 2026)

### Abstract
People rarely seek reality because it is “true.” They seek it when coherence breaks. The human default is stabilization: narratives that regulate fear, protect identity, and preserve belonging. This is not stupidity. It is structural. Collapse is the moment the stabilization system can no longer pay the cost of maintaining its story. The paper argues that collapse is not merely dysfunction; it is often forced contact—an audit event where reality pushes back hard enough that the system must either revise, harden, or fragment. Presence requires containment: the ability to hold uncertainty, contradiction, shame, and cost without converting them into premature closure. Without containment, “reality-seeking” becomes another performance—more coherence, new ideology, and a fresh insulation layer. The paper frames Structural Intelligence as the capacity to read collapse without romanticizing it, and to distinguish necessary pain (truth-load) from wasted friction (maintenance and avoidance).
