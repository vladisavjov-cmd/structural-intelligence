# Creativity and the Return of Contact: Form, Being, and the Structure of Repair

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
This paper argues that creativity is not only self-expression, novelty, or talent. At its deepest, creativity is the making of a form that can bear more reality than the old form could hold. It begins from the observation that creative work often feels more real than ordinary life, and asks why some new forms feel like a return to reality rather than an escape from it. The paper proposes that creativity begins when existing form has become too thin, too total, too captured, or too defended to carry lived reality honestly. A creative act then attempts a better holder. It distinguishes real creativity from stylish coherence, argues that creativity can function as a de-fusion practice without being identical with integration, and explains why creativity often feels like freedom: it reduces falseness by bringing life into a truer relation with form.
