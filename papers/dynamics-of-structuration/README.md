# The Dynamics of Structuration: Structural Presence, Boundary, Scale, and the Stabilization of Relation

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Journal article  
**Version:** Latest archived (Apr 2026)

### Abstract
This paper examines how structural presence, boundary, and scale interact in the stabilization of relation. It argues that structuration must be understood as a scale-sensitive process in which local formations organize burdens, mediate contact, and maintain viability within a differentiated field. Structural presence is treated not as mere existence but as adequacy: the degree to which a formation can actually hold and organize the relations it claims to carry.
