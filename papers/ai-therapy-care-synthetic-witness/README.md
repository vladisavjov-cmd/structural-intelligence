# AI Therapy, Care, and Synthetic Witness: Structural Intelligence and the Limits of Machine Care

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
This paper argues that the central problem in AI therapy is not only inaccuracy, hallucination, or unsafe advice. It is the growing capacity of current systems to produce the language, pacing, and atmosphere of care without satisfying the deeper conditions of therapeutic witness. Witness here is not passive listening or emotional fluency alone, but consequence-bearing, asymmetrical presence capable of metabolizing contradiction, carrying burden, and remaining answerable to what unfolds. On that basis, the paper introduces synthetic witness as the simulation of therapeutic holding without the full load-bearing structure of human care. It distinguishes care, therapy, and witness; explains why AI can seem unusually therapeutic; identifies structural deficits such as the asymmetry gap and textual care without a somatic floor; and clarifies what AI can still do well without being confused for real therapeutic witness.

AI therapy; machine care; synthetic witness; therapeutic witness; ethics of artificial intelligence; philosophy of psychology; human–AI interaction; transference; validation; answerability; mental health technology
