# Enactivism and Structural Intelligence: Sense-Making, Presence, and Answerability Under Pressure

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Journal article  
**Version:** Latest archived (Apr 2026)

### Abstract
This paper builds a bridge between enactivism and Structural Intelligence. Enactivism shows that cognition is embodied, situated, and enacted through the living relation between organism and world. Structural Intelligence keeps these strengths and adds a further question: when that embodied relation is strained, what remains answerable to reality, and what begins to survive through substitute control, defended coherence, or steering loss? The paper argues that SI does not replace enactivism but extends it by introducing a grammar of pressure, burden, presence, collapse, and repair. Enactivism explains how a world of significance is enacted. SI asks whether that enacted world remains load-bearing under contradiction, cost, consequence, and time. The result is a bridge that connects sense-making and embodied cognition to presence, somatic capacity, structural debt, pilot access, and answerable revision under pressure.
