# Truth Is Not Pain: Reality-Contact, Relief, and Reliability Under Constraint

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
This paper argues that reality-contact is not the same as suffering. Truth, in the practical Structural Intelligence sense, is not whatever hurts, but the reliability a form earns when it remains answerable under contradiction, consequence, time, repair, and revision. On this view, pain is neither necessary nor sufficient for truth. Some painful states are reality-bearing, while others are distorted, inherited, or coercively imposed. Likewise, some relieving states are evasive closures, while others reflect reduced falseness, better fit, and the restoration of a more adequate holder. The paper introduces somatic answerability as the body’s first registration of contact, identifies the ascetic trap as a failure mode in which suffering is mistaken for seriousness, and extends the argument across psychology, creativity, institutions, and AI.
