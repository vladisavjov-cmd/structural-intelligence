# The Diamond Core: Friction, Sentience, and Invariance in Structural Intelligence

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Journal article  
**Version:** Latest archived (Apr 2026)

### Abstract
This paper states the foundational axiom of Structural Intelligence in its strongest form. Its core claim is simple: coherence is not truth. From this follow three major relocations. First, truth is relocated from representation alone to burden-bearing answerability. In a world saturated with simulation, formal coherence is no longer enough to establish contact with reality. Truth becomes real where revision carries cost, grief, consequence, and structural change. Second, sentience is relocated from complexity alone to burden-bearing embodiment. A conscious being is not merely a processor of information, but a finite structure that can feel pressure, suffer overload, and metabolize reality from the inside. Third, sovereignty is relocated from control alone to invariance. A structure becomes truly sovereign only when it is anchored more deeply than the forms it may need to lose. These three claims form the diamond core of the wider SI corpus. Truth belongs to answerability, sentience belongs to burden-bearing, and sovereignty belongs to invariance.
