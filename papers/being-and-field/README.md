# Being and Field: Subject-Depth, Local Form, and the Non-Reduction of the Human

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (Apr 2026)

### Abstract
This paper closes the field sequence of Structural Intelligence by asking where Being sits in relation to field, local form, matter, role, output, and AI-mediated coherence. The first four papers developed the field as the differentiated condition from which local structurations emerge, described field dynamics through frequency and resonance, explained how field dynamics stabilize into local forms, and widened the topology of field through absence, dark mass, counter-form, defect, and collapse-density. This fifth paper turns to the subject. It argues that Being is not the field, not matter alone, not output, and not any local structuration. Being names the non-reducible depth of the subject within the field: the depth by which a human can inhabit, lose, revise, grieve, and outlive local forms without being identical to them. The central claim is that SI becomes fully human only when it refuses to reduce the subject to the structures through which the subject appears.
