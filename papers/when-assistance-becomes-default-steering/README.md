# When Assistance Becomes Default Steering: Structural Intelligence, Shared Answerability, and Clinical Decision Support

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (May 2026)

### Abstract
Clinical decision support is usually described as assistive rather than governing. This paper argues that under time pressure, documentation burden, workflow dependence, and institutional risk management, that distinction can collapse. A recommendation begins as support, becomes a preferred pathway, hardens into default, and then functions as authority under pressure. The paper shows how human-in-the-loop can remain formally present while answerability thins in practice, leaving the clinician as the bearer of legal, moral, and bodily consequence after real steering has already shifted. In response, it proposes a shared-answerability architecture for clinical AI, including causal anchoring, adversarial mirroring, proof of contact receipt, bounded delegation, consequence-bearing authorization, burden-path audit, and binding revision triggers.
