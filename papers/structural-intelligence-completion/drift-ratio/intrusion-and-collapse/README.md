# Intrusion and Collapse: How Deferred Contact Becomes Debt in Psyche and Society

**Author:** Vladisav Jovanović  
**Status:** Preprint  
**Version:** Latest archived (Mar 2026)

### Abstract
Structural Intelligence distinguishes coherence from contact and treats collapse as forced contact: the moment reality becomes non-optional because substitution can no longer be sustained. This paper formalizes a bridge thesis that connects individual intrusion phenomena to collapse mechanics across personal and societal scales. Intrusion is defined as occupancy of steering by a competing loop, operationalized through an action-selection mixture with time-local occupancy and time-averaged load. Structural Debt is introduced as the conserved remainder of refused contact created by substitution, and a maintenance inflation principle is added: debt is not static; the metabolic cost required to suppress returning contact rises over time, accelerating collapse. Jungian complex psychology is translated into these dynamics: a complex is an intrusion-capable structure that can seize steering under activation; the shadow is treated as the ledger of refused contact. The same mechanics scale to societies through two macro parameters: network viscosity and epistemic exchange rate. When verification becomes a survival disadvantage, systems enter a hallucination death spiral where theater is the only affordable stabilization. The paper closes with diagnostics, falsifiers, and falsifiable predictions.
